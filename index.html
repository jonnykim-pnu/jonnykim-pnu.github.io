About me

I’m Jonny Kim, a linguist specializing in experimental phonetics. I am Associate Professor at Pusan National University. My research focuses on sociophonetic variation, speech perception, and the interaction between phonetic detail and social meaning.

I am also known as Jungyun Kim (김정윤) in Korea, and publish my academic work under the name Jonny Jungyun Kim.

About my name

My mom gave me a given name Jungyun, roughly pronounced [d͡ʒ͍ʌŋ.jun]. Since I was born in Tallahassee, Florida, she also gave me an English name Jonny, which sounds a bit similar to Jungyun plus a vocative case marker /-i/ in Korean (and, of course, with some degree of phonetic reduction).

This kind of cross-linguistic wordplay likely sparked my interest in speech sounds. However, I moved to Korea at around 10 months old and have spent most of my life there, receiving all of my education in Korea except for my doctoral training. So, despite working in an English department and specializing in phonetics, my English pronunciation is far from that of native speakers. I am proud of my socio-indexical features in my pronunciation, as they are an important part of my identity!
