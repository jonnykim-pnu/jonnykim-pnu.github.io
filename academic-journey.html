Academic Journey

Ph.D.
Ph.D. in Linguistics, University of Hawai‘i at Mānoa

I received a doctoral degree at the Department of Linguistics at the University of Hawai‘i at Mānoa.

I completed my dissertation, Socially-conditioned links between words and phonetic realizations, under the supervision of Professor Katie Drager. This work examined how word recognition is influenced by socially meaningful covariance between lexical items and phonetic variants, and how socially indexed acoustic properties encountered over the course of a lifetime are remembered and represented in word-level storage.

My academic lineage is illustrated in the figure below. I have been particularly influenced by Professors Jen Hay, Katie Drager, and Abby Walker.

Postdoctoral Research
Postdoctoral Researcher, Hanyang University

My postdoctoral research explored the articulatory aspect of phonetic grammar, focusing on how low-level kinematic processes are modulated by higher-level cognitive and prosodic functions.

Using Electromagnetic Articulography (EMA), our research team examined temporal adjustment and articulatory strengthening patterns of gestural movements at different levels of rhythmic junctures.

Current Position
Associate Professor, Pusan National University

At Pusan National University, I continue my work on sociophonetics, speech perception, prosody, and phonological development, while also teaching and mentoring students in linguistics and related fields.
